Big data ETL (Extract, Transform and Load) operations typically
involve processing and managing large volumes of data, which requires significant
computing power and energy consumption. The energy-intensive nature of these
operations contributes to a substantial carbon footprint. By implementing
sustainability techniques, such as energy-efficient hardware, virtualization,
and optimized data center infrastructure, SMEs can reduce their energy consumption
and carbon emissions.
- As the world focuses on sustainability, achieving net-zero carbon emissions has become a critical goal for businesses.
- Address the challenges faced by SMEs in their journey towards net-zero carbon emissions in their ETL operations.
- Present a business model known as “Green Economy ETL Systems” that leverages technological innovation to support SMEs in reducing their carbon footprint.
